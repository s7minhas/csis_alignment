When writing or editing any prose document (vignettes, memos, reports, policy briefs, methodology sections), apply the Minhas/Dorff academic writing style. This is a binding editorial constraint, not optional guidance.

**Why:** The user has a specific, detailed academic writing style that must be enforced on all prose output. LLM-generated text tends to violate it with short formulaic paragraphs, excessive caveats, bullet-heavy narrative, em dashes, and p-value parentheses.

**How to apply:**

Core rules:
- substantive puzzle first, not abstract generalities
- methods presented as responses to substantive problems, not for their own sake
- restrained confidence (argue, show, demonstrate, estimate — never revolutionary, groundbreaking)
- every sentence must perform real analytical work; no filler
- no p-values in parentheses ever; use "statistically significant" / "distinguishable from zero" / "imprecisely measured"
- no em dashes; use commas, parentheses, or sentence breaks
- no excessive caveats in main text; move to footnotes or discussion section
- paragraphs should be 4-8 sentences developing ideas fully, not short LLM-style blocks
- no bullet lists in narrative sections
- no false precision (prefer "about 6%" over "6.3 percentage points")
- all variable names in tables/figures must be publication-ready (no code names)
- report quantities of interest (marginal effects, predicted probabilities) not raw coefficients
- audience: method-literate political scientists
- tone: disciplined, analytic, confident but not dramatic
