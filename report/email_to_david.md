**Headline findings from the updated model:**

- BRICS within-group diplomatic cohesion has risen from 0.79 (1990) to 0.92 (2024), nearly matching the G7's stable 0.93. This is a structural shift worth flagging.
- South Korea is one of the largest sustained movers toward US positions — alignment with the US rose from 0.59 to 0.86 over the period.
- Ukraine and Kazakhstan are mirror-image movers: Ukraine shifted roughly half a point toward the US since 2000, Kazakhstan the same amount toward China.
- 65 countries show a diplomacy-trade divergence — they vote one way at the UNGA but their trade dependence points the other direction. Australia is a striking case: votes with the US but sends 36% of trade to China vs 9% to the US.

**Important caveat I want to flag up front:** the diplomatic alignment scores are based on UNGA voting, which captures position-taking on international issues. The US votes in small minorities at the UNGA on many topics, so most countries appear diplomatically closer to China than to the US. This is accurate for UNGA voting but doesn't characterize the broader relationship — trade, security cooperation, and other channels tell different stories. The memo goes into this in more detail, and the dashboard has caveats on every page.
